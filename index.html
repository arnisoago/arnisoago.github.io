Modular Adaptive Segmented Telescope (MAST) Point Design

Several leading coronagraphs being developed at NASA today could be designed to work with a segmented aperture telescope, but when paired with coronagraphs telescopes must achieve extreme stability. An adaptive primary mirror demonstrating sub-angstrom levels of stability is critical for such telescopes. The Modular Adaptive Segmented Telescope (MAST) would take steps towards developing and demonstrating an ultra-stable adaptive primary mirror closed-loop electromechanical sensing and control system, as well as depositing and measuring the uniformity, polarizing, and stability of high reflectivity coatings, and enabling future high- delity end-to-end segmented aperture coronagraph demonstrations. During my time at NASA< I developed a strawman mechanical design for MAST and incorporated many of the necessary mechanical components. I performed finite element analysis to understand the deformation of mirror segments as a result of spring blocking force, as well as thermal analysis to quantify the deformation on the baseline mirror truss support structure. I also advanced work on the optical layout and mechanical design of the Radial Shearing Nulling Coronagraph (RSNC).

CubeSat Attitude Determination and Control Subsystem

During this projec It continued the development of the attitude determination and control (ADC) subsystem for a three-unit CubeSat in a high-altitude, polar, sun-synchronous orbit whose mission was to perform solar and extraterrestrial X-ray spectroscopy. I helped improve the performance of algorithms for detumble, initial attitude determination, and attitude maintenance by using MATLAB and Systems Tool Kit (STK) simulations.
